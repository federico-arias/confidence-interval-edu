Finding the mean height of a population

The purpose of this page is to teach you how to use a subset of a population to calculate a parameter from that same population.

Calculating a parameter from a population is a task we are often confronted with. We often want to know who a country is going to vote for president or what music do 17 years old listen to, but we don't have the money, nor the time to survey a whole country or every 17 year-old in the world.

So, what is the group of normally distributed values from which we are going to pick an interval? The answer is: every sample mean. We can consider every sample mean as a data point and we can plot an histogram in the same way.

If we plot all the sample means, we get a normal curve with most values in the center and few in the ends of the graph.

For example, if we take 100 samples, we'll like to say that, at least 95% of them are going to be close to the population parameter. This is what is called the confidence level, which, in our case, we set to be 95%. This is determined by researchers depending on the level of accuracy they want to achieve.
